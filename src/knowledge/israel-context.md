# Israel Sourcing Context

Everything about getting parts for a 1989 CJ8 Scrambler in Israel.

---

## The Reality

Israel is a difficult market for classic American 4x4 parts. There is no local equivalent to Rock Auto, Summit, or NAPA. The market is small, importing is expensive, and most suppliers focus on current-model vehicles.

**Cost premium vs. US pricing: 30–40%** on average for imported parts. Budget accordingly.

---

## Primary Supplier: Jeepland

**The single best local source for Jeep parts in Israel.**

- Specializes in Jeep (CJ, Wrangler, Cherokee, Grand Cherokee, Gladiator)
- Stocks both OEM and aftermarket parts
- Can order items not in stock
- Has a searchable online catalog (can be queried via API)
- Location: Israel (exact city not confirmed — verify)
- Phone/contact: look up current

**What Jeepland typically stocks for CJ8/CJ7:**
- Basic maintenance items (filters, belts, gaskets)
- Suspension parts (springs, shocks, U-bolts)
- Steering components (tie rods, drag links, steering boxes)
- Brake parts (drums, shoes, wheel cylinders, master cylinders)
- Some drivetrain (U-joints, driveshaft parts)
- Body rubber (seals, mounts)

**What Jeepland typically does NOT stock:**
- Engine rebuild parts (pistons, rings, bearings — order from US)
- AMC 258-specific machined parts
- CJ8-specific body panels (rare even in US)
- Transfer case rebuild kits
- Wiring harnesses

**Always check Jeepland first.** Even if the price is higher, avoiding import lead time is often worth it.

## Import from US — When and How

**When to import:**
- Part not available at Jeepland
- Price difference > 40% (import is competitive)
- Part is critical path and can wait 3–6 weeks
- Part is specialty / performance (Jeepland won't have it)

**Lead times (realistic, 2024–2025):**
- USPS First Class: 3–5 weeks (unreliable, sometimes 8 weeks)
- USPS Priority: 2–3 weeks
- FedEx/UPS/DHL: 1–2 weeks (expensive, ~$60–150 for typical Jeep part)
- Economy freight services (e.g. via freight forwarder): 4–6 weeks, cheaper

**Import costs:**
- Customs duty: ~12% of declared value for auto parts (verify current rate)
- VAT: 17% of (declared value + customs)
- Handling fees: ~₪100–150 per package at customs
- Effective total overhead: ~35–40% on top of US price + shipping

**Best US sources:**
- **Rock Auto:** Best prices, huge inventory, ships internationally (use freight forwarder)
- **Quadratec:** Jeep-specific, good quality control, ships direct
- **Crown Automotive:** OEM-equivalent quality, available on Amazon US
- **Omix-ADA:** Aftermarket OEM replacement, widely available
- **Morris 4x4:** Jeep specialist, good for hard-to-find items

**Freight forwarder recommendation:** Use a freight consolidator with an Israeli address in the US (e.g., Ship2Israel, BorderFree, or similar) to collect multiple orders and ship together — reduces per-package shipping costs significantly.

## Local Machine Shops

For engine work (boring, honing, line boring, etc.) and fabrication:

- There are competent machine shops in Israel but not all have experience with AMC engines
- AMC 258 pistons/rings are non-standard — must import or have machine shop source
- For welding (frame repair, body work): **shop job** — find a trusted local welder
- For wheel alignment: any alignment shop can handle a stock CJ8 setup

## Substitutions That Work in Israel

Some US-spec parts have European equivalents available locally:

| Part | US Source | Local Alternative |
|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Cooling hoses | Rock Auto | Any auto shop — cut to length |
| Belts (fan, alternator) | By size | Any auto parts store |
| Brake fluid | Any US brand | Any local brand, DOT 3 or 4 |
| Engine oil | Any US brand | Local brands fine |
| Gasket sets | Fel-Pro (import) | Victor Reinz available locally |
| Bolts/hardware | Specialty only | Local hardware store for standard sizes |
| Fuel line (rubber) | By ID/OD | Local auto shop |
| Electrical connectors | By type | Local electronics suppliers |
