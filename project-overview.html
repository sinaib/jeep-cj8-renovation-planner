Jeep CJ8 Scrambler · 1989

Restoring a classic Jeep
is not a simple project.

35 years of worn systems. Hundreds of decisions that compound on each other. Parts you can't find locally. Costs that stack up faster than you expect. Dependencies where doing the wrong thing first costs you twice.

This is the tool built to manage all of that — with an AI that knows CJ8s, builds the plan with you, and keeps watching quietly in the background.

Why this exists
The problem with restoring a classic Jeep
A spreadsheet doesn't know that you need to fix the frame before you replace the suspension. A notes app doesn't know what an AMC 20 rear axle shaft failure looks like, or that the Carter YF carburetor on a '89 CJ8 has a known idle problem. And neither one remembers what you decided about the engine rebuild three weeks ago.
🧩

Hundreds of interdependent tasks

Engine, suspension, brakes, electrical, body, frame — each system depends on others. Doing them out of order is expensive. Knowing the right order requires experience.

🔩

Parts you can't find locally

CJ8 is rarer than CJ7. Israeli market adds 30–40% premium and weeks of wait. You need to know exactly what to order before you start, not after you've already torn it apart.

🧠

Knowledge that lives in your head

Every decision made, every fact discovered, every note from the last garage session — it's scattered across WhatsApp, memory, and scraps of paper. None of it is searchable or connected.

The solution
A local AI advisor that builds the plan with you
You talk to it like a mechanic friend who happens to know CJ8s inside out. It builds phases and tasks, populates them with CJ8-specific steps and parts lists, tracks your progress, and works quietly in the background — surfacing gaps you haven't thought of, and preparing the next task the moment you finish one.

It's not a task manager.

Every task it creates comes pre-loaded: step-by-step CJ8-specific instructions, the parts you'll need with Israeli market cost estimates, and the reason the task exists. It fills in the knowledge, not just the structure.


It's not a chatbot.

It writes to a persistent plan. When it adds a task, that task lives in your project — with steps, parts, costs, and notes — not just in the chat history. The conversation feeds the plan.


It's local and private.

Everything runs on your machine. The plan is saved to disk. The only thing that goes to the internet is your messages to Claude. No cloud backend, no accounts, no sync services.

Step by step
How a session works
You don't fill in a form. You just talk. The AI figures out what needs doing and structures it.
Step 1

You describe your Jeep

Engine condition, mileage, what you've already done, your skills, your budget, your goals. The AI records every confirmed fact and uses it to shape every recommendation from that point on. It won't suggest a full engine rebuild if you've told it the engine is fine.

Step 2

The AI builds a structured plan

It creates phases (Safety First, Engine Revival, Suspension & Axles...) and fills each with tasks. Every task it creates is immediately populated: step-by-step CJ8-specific instructions, required parts with costs in ₪, and the rationale for why this task exists at all.

Step 3

You work the plan, it tracks progress

Mark tasks active, done, or skipped. Add notes from your garage sessions. Upload photos and get AI technical observations. The AI adjusts its recommendations as your actual situation becomes clearer.

Step 4

The AI works in the background

Even when you're not in the chat, it's watching. When you complete a task, it automatically prepares the next ones. Every week it scans for stale work and structural gaps. Whenever the plan changes, it checks for CJ8-specific risks you might have missed.

Tech stack
What it's built with
All local. No cloud backend. The Anthropic SDK runs in-browser with a local API key.
UI

Component-based. Framer Motion for animations. Single page, view-switched.
State

Persist middleware with custom dual-write adapter (disk + localStorage simultaneously).

Dev Server
Vite + Node.js Middleware
Custom configureServer hook adds 5 fs-backed API routes. No separate process.
Files

Binary blobs (photos, PDFs) in IndexedDB. File metadata in Zustand + disk.
