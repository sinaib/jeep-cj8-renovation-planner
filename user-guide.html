01
Your first session — what to tell it
The agent builds a mental model of your Jeep. The more context you give upfront, the better every future answer will be.
🧠
The agent remembers forever. You only have to tell it something once. It stores key facts about your Jeep in a car profile — engine condition, mileage, skills, budget, goals — and refers back to them when planning tasks or suggesting priorities.

Start a fresh session and send a message like this — be as specific as you can:

✦ Great first message
Hi. I have a 1989 Jeep CJ8 Scrambler with the AMC 258 I-6. It has about 180,000 km on it. The engine runs but smokes on cold start and has a rough idle. The transfer case is fine, axles were rebuilt 3 years ago. Brakes haven't been touched in years. Body has surface rust on the sills and one small patch on the driver floor. I'm intermediate skill level — comfortable with tools, have done brakes and oil changes but never engine work. My budget is around ₪70,000 total. Goal is a reliable daily driver that can handle some weekend trails. Where should I start?

After this, the agent will record all those facts and propose your first phases. You'll see it call tools in the background — that's it building the plan.

🔑

Facts it wants to know

Engine condition, mileage, known issues, which systems were recently worked on, body rust situation, transmission type.

🧰

About you

Your DIY skill level, tools you have access to, whether you have a lift, and how much time you can dedicate per week.

🎯

Your goals

Daily driver, trail rig, show-quality restoration, or somewhere in between. This shapes what "done" looks like for every task.

💰

Budget

Tell it your total budget in ₪. It will flag when estimates are approaching or exceeding it, and suggest lower-cost alternatives.

02
How plan building works
The agent creates phases and tasks — each with step-by-step instructions and Israeli market parts estimates.
💬

You describe what you know

Tell the agent about your Jeep's condition, what you've noticed, what's next. No need to be technical — describe symptoms.

🔍

Agent researches and reasons

It searches for CJ8-specific information, cross-references known failure points for the AMC 258, and builds tasks that are specific to your Jeep — not a generic guide.

📦

Phases and tasks appear

The agent creates phases in logical order, adds tasks with priorities, records step-by-step how-to guides, and estimates costs in ₪ from Israeli market sources.

🔗

Dependencies are set automatically

It knows that you must fix the rear main seal before painting the frame, and that brakes come before trail driving. These ordering constraints are tracked so nothing gets skipped.

⚠️

Gaps appear quietly

While you work, the agent silently scans for things you might have missed — CJ8 brake fade at altitude, rust behind the spring hangers, missing ground straps. These show as orange flags.

06
What the agent does on its own
Even when you're not chatting, three background processes are quietly watching your plan.
🔄

After any change — Gap scan

8 seconds after you or the agent modifies the plan, a quiet scan runs. It checks for CJ8-specific gaps you might have missed — things like the vacuum advance on the distributor being connected, or timing chain stretch at this mileage.

Result: orange warning flags appear in your plan.

✅

When you complete a task

When a task is marked done, the agent checks if any other tasks just became unblocked. If so, it pre-populates their parts lists and step-by-step guides so you're ready to start immediately.

Result: newly unblocked tasks arrive fully detailed.

📅

Weekly — Health check

Once a week on app load, it scans for stale tasks (marked "active" but no recent progress notes), overloaded phases, and missing restoration phases that a complete CJ8 rebuild typically needs.

Result: flags for stale tasks and structural plan issues.

🔇
All background activity is silent. You won't see a message or get interrupted. Gaps and notes just appear in the plan. Look for orange warning triangles — those are the background agent's contributions.
07
Tips, traps, and habits that help
The patterns that separate a good plan from a great one.
✓ DO THIS
  • Tell the agent what you found when you inspect something, not just what you planned to do.
  • Log actual costs when you finish each task — the estimate vs. actual gap tells you where the plan was wrong.
  • Ask "what am I missing?" regularly. The agent knows common CJ8 failure patterns you might not.
  • Use it to record decisions, even minor ones. "Decided to use Redline MT-90 in the transfer case."
  • Upload photos of rust, wear, or any "what is this?" moments in the garage.
  • Ask for step-by-step instructions before starting anything unfamiliar — it will write CJ8-specific guides.
✗ AVOID THIS
  • Don't ask vague questions like "tell me about CJ8 engines" — be specific to your car and your plan.
  • Don't skip logging completions. The plan becomes stale and the weekly check will flag everything as abandoned.
  • Don't ignore the orange warning flags — the agent only raises them when it thinks something is important.
  • Don't add tasks yourself without telling the agent why — it can't set dependencies or add steps if it doesn't know the context.
  • Don't share budget info vaguely. "I have some money" doesn't help. "₪70,000 total, ₪20,000 available now" is useful.
The single best habit
💬
Talk to it like it was there in the garage with you. "Pulled the valve cover — three of the valve seals are shot and there's sludge on the cam. What does this tell us about the engine's maintenance history and what should we add to the plan?" This kind of message gives the agent what it needs to reason, not just retrieve.
Understanding response speed
Fast (instant) Simple questions, greetings, status checks, "what's next?" — routed to a lightweight model, no tools, responds in under 2 seconds.
Full (5–20 sec) Anything involving research, adding tasks, updating the plan, analyzing photos, or calculating costs — uses the full AI with access to all 23 tools. You'll see the tool calls appear as it works.
Data safety

Everything is stored locally on your computer — the full plan, all conversation history, every photo, every cost estimate. Nothing goes to any server except the AI API calls themselves (which don't store your plan data). The plan is saved as a plain project.json file on disk, backed up to a changelog.ndjson audit log, and synced to localStorage. Even if the app crashes, nothing is lost.

Quick reference
Task statuses, flags, and what they mean.
FLAGGED Something the agent noticed that needs your attention — a possible issue, an inspection needed, or a task the agent can't fully define without you looking first.
TODO Defined, scheduled, not started. Dependencies may or may not be complete — check before starting.
ACTIVE You're currently working on this. Add notes as you go — the weekly check will flag active tasks with no notes as potentially stale.
DONE Complete. The actual cost should be logged. Completing a task may unlock dependent tasks — the agent will populate them automatically.
Warning flag severities
🔴 Critical Safety risk or something that will cause a bigger problem if ignored. Address before the next drive.
🟠 Warning Important gap in the plan — not immediately dangerous but should be addressed in the current phase.
🔵 Suggestion Something worth considering — a nice-to-have, a more efficient order, or a preventative measure.
