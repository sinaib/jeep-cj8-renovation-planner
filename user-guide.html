First Time

Getting started

Onboarding is a conversation. The advisor asks about your car and situation to build a starting profile. There's no form to fill — just talk.

1
Describe where the car is right now
Don't worry about getting it perfect. The advisor needs to know the current state — running, disassembled, stalled mid-project, sitting for years. Be specific about what you know and honest about what you don't.
EXAMPLE
"I took it apart 8 years ago for a full redo. Got halfway, stopped. Some parts are off, some on, I have a pile of new old-stock parts I bought. I'm not sure what works and what doesn't."
2
Answer the advisor's follow-up questions
It'll ask about the engine (AMC 150 2.5L or AMC 258 4.2L?), transmission, your budget range, whether you're doing the work yourself, what you have for tools, and what you ultimately want to use the car for. These answers shape every recommendation.
3
Let it build the first phases
The advisor will create phases and tasks based on what you told it — starting from where you actually are. Phase 1 is almost always Assessment & Inventory because you can't plan until you know what you have.
4
You're in the main interface
Your plan is on the left, the advisor is on the right — always. You can now navigate phases, open tasks, ask questions, request more detail on any topic, or just start working.
Note: Onboarding only runs once. If you want to re-run it, you can reset the app from Settings → Reset App. Your existing data will be cleared.

Plan Column Tabs

Three ways to look at your work

The PLAN / WORK NOW / JOURNEY tabs give you three different perspectives on the same data.

PLAN
The full map
All phases as cards, in order. Expand any phase to see its tasks. The colored top band shows phase status — amber for in-progress, green for complete, red for critical. This is the default view.
Use when: reviewing the overall plan, looking for what's in a specific phase, understanding the scope.
WORK NOW
What to do next
A flat sorted list of all incomplete tasks — ordered by priority (critical first), then phase order. Shows which tasks are blocked (dependencies not met) vs available to start. Has quick START and ✓ (done) buttons.
Use when: sitting down to work, deciding what to tackle today, seeing the full critical-path view.
JOURNEY
What you've done
Chronological log of completed tasks, newest first. Shows notes, photos, actual vs estimated cost. Also shows key decisions recorded by the advisor in a summary block at the top.
Use when: tracking progress, reviewing what was done before a session, documenting the restoration story.

Conversations

How to talk to the advisor

The advisor is a mechanic who knows your car and your plan. Talk to it like one. These examples show what kind of conversations it can handle.

💰 Asking about costs and sourcing
You
What should a brake master cylinder rebuild cost me? Check jeepland.
Advisor
Found it on Jeepland — rebuild kit is ₪220, new aftermarket unit ₪480. If yours is more than 10 years old since last replacement, go new. The original master on an '89 is almost certainly original.
✓ search_jeepland: "CJ8 brake master cylinder"
✓ update_task_cost: Brake master cylinder → ₪480
🔩 Getting a step-by-step guide
You
Write me the step-by-step for checking the AMC 258 head gasket condition without pulling the head.
Advisor
For the AMC 258, there are four signs you can check without disassembly: oil fill cap (white mayonnaise = coolant in oil), coolant reservoir (oil sheen), compression test across all 6 cylinders (a dead or low cylinder adjacent to the hot side is a classic sign on the 258), and idle behavior — the 258 misfires distinctively when a head gasket is seeping. I've saved the full procedure to the task.
✓ set_task_steps: "Check engine condition" → 8 steps
📦 Building out a phase
You
Map out all the tasks I'll need for the frame and body mounts phase.
Advisor
Added 7 tasks to the Frame & Body Mounts phase. The CJ8 has 8 body mount positions — 4 more than the CJ7 because of the longer wheelbase. The rear crossmember is the one most likely to have rusted through. I flagged it as critical since structural integrity is a safety issue.
✓ add_task: "Inspect all 8 body mount positions" → Frame phase · CRITICAL
✓ add_task: "Measure rear crossmember thickness" → Frame phase · CRITICAL
✓ add_task: "Source body mount bushings" → Frame phase · HIGH
✓ flag_gap: "No frame rust treatment task" [critical]
🏁 Marking something done
You
I finished inventorying all the parts. Found 3 boxes of NOS parts I forgot about — brake hardware, some engine bolts, and a complete fuel line kit.
Advisor
Good find. NOS fuel line kit will save you around ₪350 at current prices. I've marked the inventory task done and recorded the discovery. Those engine bolts — AMC 258 head bolts are torque-to-yield on some years, worth identifying them specifically.
✓ update_task_status: "Inventory all removed parts" → done
✓ add_task_note: "Found NOS: brake hardware, engine bolts, complete fuel line kit"
✓ add_research_note: "NOS fuel line kit found in inventory, saves ~₪350"
What the advisor won't do: give vague generic advice, pretend to know something it doesn't, give you a cost estimate without checking jeepland, or plan based on a CJ7 when you have a CJ8. If it's uncertain, it says so and searches.
Task Detail View

Inside a task

Click any task row to open the Task Detail view. The plan column is replaced by a deep-dive on that one task. Press Back to return.

✅
What you'll find in a task
🔄
Status buttons — quickly change to todo / active / done / flagged / skipped directly from this view.
📋
Step-by-step guide — if the advisor has written steps for this task, they appear as a numbered checklist. Specific to the CJ8 Scrambler.
🔩
Parts list — required parts with part numbers, suppliers, cost, and purchased status. Can be expanded via the advisor.
📝
Notes — advisor observations and your discoveries. Timestamped. Ask the advisor to add notes, or it adds them automatically as you complete related tasks.
📸
Photo gallery — photos attached to this task. See below for how to add them.
₪
Cost tracking — estimated vs actual cost. Update actual cost after completing the work.
Tip: When you're viewing a task and ask the advisor a question, it automatically includes the task context — task name, phase, system. You don't need to specify which task you mean.
Photo Attachment

Show the advisor what you're looking at

Photos are the fastest way to get a specific assessment. The advisor can see corrosion, damage, part condition, and identify what you're looking at.

1
Open the relevant task
The paperclip icon (📎) in the advisor's input box only appears when you're viewing a task. This ensures photos are linked to the right place in your plan.
2
Tap the paperclip and select your photo
A preview thumbnail appears above the input. You can ask a specific question with it, or just send the photo alone — the advisor will describe what it sees.
EXAMPLE
Send a photo of the frame rail with the message: "Is this rust through or just surface corrosion?"
3
Photo is stored in the task automatically
The image is stored in your browser's IndexedDB (no server upload, no account needed). It appears in the task's photo gallery and in the JOURNEY view for that task. The advisor's analysis is saved as an annotation on the file.
Silent AI Activity

What happens without you asking

The advisor does three things automatically, in the background, without adding messages to the conversation. You'll see their results as new gap flags and task updates.

🔍
Automatic Gap Scan
After plan changes
⏱
Fires 8 seconds after you or the advisor makes a plan change (add/edit tasks or phases), but only if 10+ minutes have passed since the last scan.
🚨
Checks the entire plan against known CJ8 failure points, missing safety tasks, and uncaptured dependencies. Adds critical gaps to the counter in the top bar.
💬
Does not add any chat messages — silent. You'll just notice the gap count change.
✅
Post-Completion Analysis
When a task is marked done
🔓
Immediately when you mark a task done, the advisor checks what tasks are now unblocked.
📋
Writes step-by-step guides into the newly-unblocked tasks so they're ready when you get there.
🔩
Populates parts lists for the next tasks, so you know what to order.
📅
Weekly Health Check
On app load, max once per week
🗂
Checks for a complete CJ8 restoration phase coverage — flags if major systems (brakes, electrical, frame) have no phase.
⏰
Flags tasks that have been "active" for a long time with no notes — prompts you to update their status.
📦
Warns if any phase has more than 10 tasks — suggests splitting it for clarity.
Settings Panel

Configuration & export

Access from the ⚙ icon in the top right.

🔑
API Key
Your Anthropic API key. Stored locally in the browser, never sent anywhere except directly to the Anthropic API. Get one at console.anthropic.com. Alternatively, set VITE_ANTHROPIC_API_KEY in .env.local for development.
📤
Export Plan
Downloads your complete plan as a JSON file — all phases, tasks, notes, decisions, carFacts, research notes. Useful as a backup or for sharing with a mechanic.
🗑
Reset App
Clears all data and returns to onboarding. Use this if you want to start fresh. Export your plan first if you want to keep a copy.
Getting the Most Out of It

Pro tips

Patterns that make the advisor significantly more useful.

📸
Photo first, questions second
Before asking about a specific component's condition, attach a photo. A photo gives the advisor context that takes paragraphs to describe in words.
"Is this rust on the body mount serious?" + photo → instant condition assessment
🇮🇱
Ask about jeepland explicitly
The advisor can search jeepland.co.il directly. When discussing parts, ask it to check jeepland for current stock and pricing — it'll update the task cost estimates automatically.
"Check jeepland for AMC 258 carburetor pricing"
📋
Ask for steps before you start
Before starting any task, open it and ask the advisor to write the step-by-step procedure for your specific car. It will be saved into the task for reference while you work.
"Write me the steps for this task on a CJ8 Scrambler with a Dana 300"
💬
Talk through discoveries
When you find something unexpected — corrosion, wrong parts, surprises — tell the advisor. It will add a note, adjust cost estimates, and check if this changes any dependencies.
"Found that the rear crossmember has a 3cm crack welded badly by a previous owner"
🗺
Use WORK NOW before each session
Before sitting down to work, switch to the WORK NOW tab. It shows exactly what's available right now, ordered by priority, with blocked tasks grayed out. No hunting through phases.
⚠
Take critical gaps seriously
The red critical badge in the top bar is the advisor flagging something it knows matters. Click it to jump to the critical task. These are usually safety-related or structural — don't dismiss them without reading.
🏛
Record decisions explicitly
When you make a major choice — budget limit, build direction, which supplier to use — tell the advisor. It will record it as a decision that shapes all future recommendations.
"We're going for a reliable daily driver, budget is ₪25,000 total, DIY everything except welding"
🔧
Ask it to phase-map the whole build
If you want a complete restoration plan quickly, ask the advisor to map out all the phases your CJ8 needs from where it is now to the goal. It will create a structured multi-phase plan in one go.
"Map out all the phases needed to take my CJ8 from disassembled to running and roadworthy"
Remember: The advisor reads your whole plan before answering complex questions (using the get_full_plan tool). It knows the state of every task, every gap, every decision recorded. The more context you give it about your car and situation, the more specific and useful its answers.
